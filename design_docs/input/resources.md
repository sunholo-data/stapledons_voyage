üß´ 3a. Alien Biosphere Risks: Science vs Tropes

To maintain Grounded Strangeness, alien biosphere interaction should follow real science, not sci-fi movie tropes.

**Real Risks (include these):**

| Risk | Scientific Basis |
|------|------------------|
| Biochemical incompatibility | Alien proteins may use D-amino acids (Earth uses L-). Completely indigestible, nutritionally useless. |
| Allergenic compounds | Novel organic molecules can trigger immune responses. Real risk with any foreign biomaterial. |
| Prion-like misfolding | If alien life uses proteins, misfolded versions could theoretically propagate in compatible substrates. |
| Ecosystem contamination | Earth microbes could devastate alien ecosystems, or vice versa. The greater ethical/practical risk. |
| Useless chirality | Alien sugars/proteins with wrong handedness provide zero nutrition despite appearing edible. |

**Sci-Fi Tropes (avoid or subvert):**

| Trope | Why It's Unrealistic |
|-------|---------------------|
| Alien plague instantly kills crew | Pathogens evolve for specific host biochemistry. Alien viruses wouldn't "know" how to infect human cells. |
| Perfect alien parasites | Evolution is host-specific. No alien organism would be pre-adapted to human biology. |
| Instant toxic atmosphere death | Real atmospheric hazards cause damage over time, not movie-style instant collapse. |
| "The alien virus is adapting!" | Viral adaptation takes generations, not hours. Dramatically convenient but scientifically nonsense. |

**Gameplay Implications:**

The interesting tension isn't "alien disease kills everyone" ‚Äî it's:
- **Biochemical lottery**: Sample alien life ‚Üí might be useless (wrong chirality), might be goldmine
- **Contamination ethics**: Our bacteria could devastate their ecosystem. Is harvesting worth the risk to them?
- **Ecosystem competition**: Alien microbes might outcompete our food crops if brought aboard
- **Long-term unknowns**: Effects may not manifest for years (subtle immune issues, slow ecosystem drift)

This creates meaningful choices without relying on Hollywood plague scenarios.
