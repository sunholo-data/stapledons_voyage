# Interview Questions Bank

Questions organized by topic for vision interviews.

## Core Experience

### Emotional Arc
- "When a player finishes Stapledon's Voyage, what should they feel?"
- "What's the single moment you want players to remember a week later?"
- "Should players feel powerful, humbled, or something else?"

### Hook & Loop
- "What's the 'one more turn' hook for this game?"
- "What keeps players engaged across a multi-hour session?"
- "What makes them want to start a new game after finishing?"

### Endings
- "What makes a 'good' ending vs a 'bad' ending?"
- "Should there be clear win/lose states or something more nuanced?"
- "How should the legacy report make players feel about their choices?"

## Mechanics

### Essential vs Nice-to-Have
- "What mechanics are absolutely essential for this game to work?"
- "What mechanics would you cut last if you had to?"
- "What mechanics are tempting but actually don't belong?"

### Difficulty & Friction
- "What should feel hard and why?"
- "What should feel easy and why?"
- "Where should players struggle meaningfully vs have smooth flow?"

### Agency & Acceptance
- "Where should players feel in control?"
- "Where should players feel like they're accepting consequences?"
- "What's the balance between player agency and accepting the universe's rules?"

### Irreversibility
- "What decisions should be truly irreversible?"
- "Should players be able to 'take back' anything?"
- "How do you prevent save-scumming from undermining the experience?"

## Narrative

### Story Structure
- "How much authored story vs emergent narrative?"
- "Are there scripted events or is everything simulated?"
- "What role does player character have in the narrative?"

### Themes
- "What themes must be present?"
- "What should the game say about humanity?"
- "What should the game say about civilization and time?"

### Alien Minds
- "How alien should the aliens be?"
- "Can players truly understand non-human minds?"
- "What makes contact meaningful vs just transactional?"

## The Ship

### Crew
- "What's the player's relationship with their crew?"
- "How much do individual crew members matter?"
- "What happens to the ship over generations?"

### Resources & Limits
- "What resources are scarce?"
- "What are the hard limits on the ship?"
- "How does the ship change over the 100 years?"

## Civilizations

### Simulation Depth
- "How detailed should civilization simulation be?"
- "What aspects of civilization are most important to model?"
- "Should players be able to predict civilization trajectories?"

### Interaction
- "What can players actually do with civilizations?"
- "What changes when players interact vs leave them alone?"
- "How much influence should players have?"

## Time & Scale

### Time Dilation
- "How should time dilation feel to play?"
- "What makes the time passage meaningful, not just a number?"
- "How do you make millennia feel weighty?"

### The Million Year Mark
- "What should Year 1,000,000 reveal?"
- "How does the final fast-forward feel?"
- "What makes the legacy report satisfying?"

## Scope & Constraints

### MVP
- "What's the minimum viable version of this game?"
- "What features would you ship in an 'Early Access' version?"
- "What can be added post-launch?"

### Out of Scope
- "What's explicitly NOT in this game?"
- "What similar games does this intentionally NOT be?"
- "What player expectations should we subvert?"

### Technical
- "Any constraints from the AILANG integration?"
- "What's the performance envelope?"
- "What platforms matter?"

## Follow-Up Questions

Use these to dig deeper after any answer:

- "Why is that important to you?"
- "Can you give an example of a game that does this well?"
- "Can you give an example of a game that gets this wrong?"
- "How would you know if we got this right?"
- "What would break if we didn't have this?"
- "Is this a pillar or a feature?"
